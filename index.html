Elimina Castle

Elmina Castle was erected by the Portuguese in 1482 as São Jorge da Mina Castle, also known simply as Mina or Feitoria da Mina in present-day Elmina, Ghana.

Osu Castle

Osu Castle, also known as Fort Christiansborg or simply the Castle, is a castle located in Osu, Accra, Ghana on the coast of the Atlantic Ocean's Gulf of Guinea

Fort Coenraadsburg

Fort Coenraadsburg or Conraadsburg, also Fort São Tiago da Mina, is a fort on the Dutch Gold Coast, built in 1652 to protect Fort Elmina from attacks.

Cape Coast Castle

Cape Coast Castle is one of about forty "slave castles", or large commercial forts, built on the Gold Coast of West Africa by European traders.

Mole National Park

It is Ghana's largest, oldest and best game viewing park. While elephants live in many remote places in Ghana, it is only at Mole National Park where you can expect to see elephants in Ghana

Bui National Park

The third-largest national park in Ghana and a home to the most prominent of these is the large hippopotamus and a large number of antelope, monkeys and birds that inhabit the 1800 square kilometer.

Picathartes Bird

Over 760 bird species, more than 1000 butterfly species, and an interesting diversity of flora and mammals. The picathartes, rockfowl or bald crows are a small genus of two passerine bird species.

Boabeng-Fiema Monkey Sanctuary

It is one of Ghana’s most distinguished examples of Traditional African Conservation Practice site having Colobus vellerosus and Cercopithecus campbelli species

Chieftancy In Ghana

Chiefs are the traditional leaders in most Ghanaian society, there serves as leaders, mediators and head of a traditional clan.

Traditional Beads

Beads are made from glass, brass, bauxite, gold and shells.Beads from all over the world are available in the Ghanaian markets.

Dipo Dance

Dipo ceremony celebrates the transition period of a child to adulthood among the people Krobos of Ghana.

Chalewote Street Festival

The Chale Wote is the biggest Street Art Festival which brings art, music and performance to the streets of Jamestown, a suburb of Accra.

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

Kwame Nkrumah Park features bronze statues and shaped fountains, dedicated earlier in 1990s to the first president of Ghana and a hub to curios collection of properties of Nkrumah

Kente Cloth Weaving

The kente cloth is traditionally woven on a narrow horizontal wood structure called a loom. A heddle is an integral part of a loom.

Larabanga Mosque

It is the oldest mosque in the country built in the Sudanese architectural style and is one of the oldest in West Africa, and has been referred to as the "Mecca of West Africa" since 1421

Akuaba dolls

Akuaba dolls are wooden carved fertility dolls and are a general symbol of good luck and health. They are known the best from the Ashanti people from Ghana. Traditionally a woman would wear the Akuaba on her back hoping to conceive a child or that her current child would be born healthy.

TALKING DRUM

The talking drum is used to send information to the rural people of Ghana, each note symbolizes the type of message and the authority. In the traditional Ghanaian society it is advisable to obey the sound of the drum and the Gongo beater (Drummer).

Kente cloth with its shapes and colors has a hidden meaning to an African. Its color can be used depending on the ceremony, it could be for royalty(gold),wedding(blue),naming ceremony(white),church(white & blue), and funeral(black).
